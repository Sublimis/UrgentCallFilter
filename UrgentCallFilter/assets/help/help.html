How does it work?

Imagine yourself desperately wanting to reach somebody by phone but there is no answer, either because the person is sleeping, simply forgot to turn the ringer tone on, or can't hear it because ring volume is too low. You would keep calling and calling — depending on how important reaching this person is for you. If it's important, you would've call many times in a matter of minutes.

Urgent Call Filter smartly recognizes this kind of situations, and enables audible ringtone (or boosts it's volume) for the incoming call that's considered important, based on your preferences. You may want to be fairly easily reachable while asleep, or you may want only very, very important calls to disturb you from your peaceful activities.

It does so without using system resources, and without running in the background all the time. Thus it is very reliable, and one of set-it-and-forget-it kind of applications.

Note: By default, app only works in Silent mode (ringer turned off) — out of Silent mode, it will do nothing. This can be changed by preference.

Target audience

This app is intended for people who often set their phones to Silent mode (ie. turn the ringer tone off), or turn the ringer volume way down — for example while sleeping — yet still want to be reachable for important situations.

It's not intended for those who use Silent mode only when in meetings, lectures or similar do-not-disturb situations of short duration, where unattended ringtone escalation would cause inconvenience.

What is call intensity?

Call intensity is the number of rings in a (short) time period.

As an example, consider person A that calls you three times in five minutes, and person B that calls you six times in five minutes. We say that calls from person B are more intensive in this case. Person C, who would call six times in 10 minutes, would have the same call intensity as person A.

Intensity levels

Low (1/4)
Incoming call ring will be audible only if the last 1 call had enough persistence.

Medium (2/4)
Incoming call ring will be audible only if the last 2 calls had enough persistence.

High (3/4)
Incoming call ring will be audible only if the last 3 calls had enough persistence.

Extreme (4/4)
Incoming call ring will be audible only if the last 4 calls had enough persistence.

Note: By default, app does nothing when phone is not in Silent mode. This can be changed by preference.

What is call persistence?

Call persistence is the density of rings over a (short) time period, taking ring duration into account.

As an example, consider person A that calls you three times in five minutes, each time ringing for 20 seconds, and person B that calls you three times in five minutes, but each time ringing for a whole minute. We say that calls from person B are more persistent in this case. Person C, who would call six times in five minutes by ringing for 10 seconds each time, would have the same call persistence as person A.

Applicable ringer modes

By default, app will do it's magic only when the ringer is in Silent mode, ie. ringtone turned off.

This can be changed by preference to also include Vibrate and Sound modes, or a combination of modes. It can also be set to work in any mode — Silent, Vibrate, and Sound.

When in Sound mode, app will behave a bit different than normal. Since the ringer is already turned on (and there's no point of turning it on again), app will instead boost the ringtone volume of important call to 100%.

Important thing to remember here is that the volume of important call will always be boosted to the maximum when app is set to work in Sound mode and the phone is indeed in Sound mode, regardless of the Volume setting.

This is useful when ringtone is not loud enough for the person to hear it, for example in noisy environments, or when the phone is in the other room, etc.
